OMAR ALMATOV

I grew up in Almaty, Kazakhstan and now I live in SF.

Interested in data sovereigntyJust like water, timber, oil, and gas — data is a valuable resource. One that should be protected and treated sovereignly and sustainably. Data sovereignty for the individual means to having control over how your data is used, stored, and shared. The early internet was built this way. This website, for instance, is statically generated. It lives on my disk. If I'm fed up with how Neocities is run, I can take it to a different host. I can even run it on my own hardware. Nobody owns it but me. At some point our data got stuck in platforms and was weaponized against us to control and surveil us, to sell us back to ourselves. But it doesn't have to be this way. There are people building sustainable, earth-friendly infrastructure today. Technology doesn't have to suck. The second law of thermodynamics isn't going anywhere, but we've managed to live with entropy for thousands of years. Hope this inspires who ever it inspires to hop off platforms and make their own website. and permacomputing.

In my free time I like to write poems, climb rocks, go alone to the beach, and I recently started figure drawing.
